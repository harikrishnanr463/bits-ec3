General EC3 Exam Strategy

1

Do All Previous Year Papers First

This is the single highest-ROI action. PYQs reveal the examiner's style, high-frequency topics, and question depth. Do every available Mid-Sem and End-Sem paper for each subject. Patterns repeat.

2

Timed Practice Is Non-Negotiable

Solve at least 2 full mock tests under timed conditions (3 hours). This trains your brain for exam-day speed. Without timing, you won't know if you're actually exam-ready.

3

Start Answers with What You Know

Even if you're unsure, write the relevant formula, the setup, and what you can derive. Partial marks are awarded for methodology. Never leave a question blank.

4

The 40-40-20 Review Rule

Spend 40% of revision time on your weakest topic, 40% on practice problems across all topics, and 20% on quick reviews of strong topics. Don't over-study what you already know.

5

On Exam Day: Read Everything First

Spend the first 5-8 minutes reading all questions. Mark easy, medium, and hard ones. Answer in order: easy ‚Üí medium ‚Üí hard. This ensures maximum marks from high-confidence areas.
